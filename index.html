Overview


As the United States population continues to grow, the population of individuals with disabilities is growing at a faster rate than the general population. This unusual growth in persons with disabilities is being exacerbated by the increase in disabilities caused by drug abuse, both legal and illegal, autism, ADD and ADHD.

Unfortunately, existing local, state, and federal programs currently offered, and the funding associated with these existing programs, are not able to keep pace with the recent trends and the demand for resources leading to a backlog of people needing services.

Mission


Our mission is to provide a better life to the disabled population. Right now families and individuals wait 5 to 10 years to receive funding from government agencies. The goal of Tender Mercies is to provide immediate assistance. This assistance will provide necessary interim funding for qualifying families with disabled children, who are not currently able to receive the assistance they so desperately need now.

Tender Mercies offers hope to families in dire needs as we partner with local service providers who specialize in working with persons with disabilities to meet their needs.

Services may include long-term residential 24-hour supervision and care; short-term respite care; daily living supports, day programs, afterschool programs, education, trade schools, teaching institutions. We also assist with any other supports and services, such as seasoned professionals whose talents and resources have the potential to enrich the lives of the disabled population.

Services


Tender Mercies helps children and families to be as successful as possible. We do this by offering the following:

  • Assistance with one-time emergency placement support
  • Assistance with on-going services for children needing part-time, or full-time supervision, and residential support
  • Assistance with community support
  • Assistance and expert guidance when requesting state services as needed
  • Assistance with daily living skills and daily needs
  • Grants to provider agencies to assist in further care of the individuals currently in services
  • Grants for individuals in services to meet wishes or desires wants and needs
  • Assistance with medication administering
